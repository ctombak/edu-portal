AI fluency is now a core leadership competency, not an optional add-on. Leaders who can leverage AI for themselves and enable their teams to do the same will outperform those who can't.

## The AI-Enabled Leader

How leaders can use AI today: Decision Support (summarizing data, scenario modeling), Communication (drafting, translation, tone adjustment), Meeting Prep (research synthesis, agenda generation), Coaching Support (role-playing difficult conversations), Strategic Thinking (devil's advocate, assumption testing). The 30% Rule: everyone needs at least 30% AI fluency—enough to use tools, ask good questions, interpret outputs, and redesign work.

> Managers aren't being replaced by AI; they are being replaced by managers who know how to use AI.

**Framework: AI Leadership Applications** — Decision support, Communication, Meeting prep, Coaching support, Strategic thinking. Everyone needs 30% AI fluency.

## Building an AI-Ready Team

The leader's three roles: Permission (explicitly tell your team it's OK to use AI—many fear 'cheating' stigma), Protection (create guardrails—what's off-limits: confidential data, final decisions without human review), Practice (allocate experimentation time: 'Spend 30 minutes this week trying AI on one repetitive task'). The Adoption Curve: Pioneers (10%—make them internal champions), Pragmatists (60%—give specific use cases, pair with pioneers), Skeptics (30%—address concerns, show 'AI + Human' examples).

**Framework: AI Team Readiness** — Permission (it's OK to use AI), Protection (guardrails for safety), Practice (experimentation time). Map team on adoption curve: Pioneers, Pragmatists, Skeptics.

## The Human Edge

What AI cannot replace: Judgment in Ambiguity (AI gives options; leaders choose based on values), Relational Trust (people follow people who see them, not algorithms), Accountability (AI suggests; humans own outcomes), Ethical Navigation (when 'efficient' isn't 'right').
