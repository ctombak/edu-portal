In flattening organizations, positional authority matters less. The ability to influence without formal power—through relationships, credibility, and strategic communication—becomes essential.

## The Trust Equation & Relational Capital

From David Maister, Charles Green & Robert Galford's research: Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation. Credibility comes from expertise and track record. Reliability from consistency and follow-through. Intimacy from safety, discretion, and empathy. Self-Orientation is the denominator—it amplifies or diminishes everything else. You can be highly credible and reliable but still not trusted if people sense you're primarily in it for yourself.

> You can be highly credible and reliable but still not trusted if people sense you're primarily in it for yourself.

**Framework: The Trust Equation** — Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation. Self-orientation is the denominator—high self-orientation kills trust regardless of other factors.

## Stakeholder Mapping & Engagement

For any initiative: identify who is directly affected, who has decision-making authority, who has informal influence, who controls resources, and who could block progress. Analyze each stakeholder's interests, current position, concerns, and influence level. Map them on the Influence/Engagement Grid to determine your engagement strategy: Partner (high influence, high engagement), Involve (high influence, low engagement), Show Consideration (low influence, high engagement), Monitor (low influence, low engagement).

**Framework: Stakeholder Analysis Grid** — Map stakeholders on Influence vs. Engagement: Partner (collaborate), Involve (consult), Show Consideration, Monitor (inform).

## Navigating Political Dynamics

Politics isn't a dirty word—it's how decisions get made and resources get allocated. Principles for ethical political navigation: understand the game but stay true to your values, build alliances based on shared interests, don't make permanent enemies, manage your political capital wisely, and build coalitions before you need them.

## Strategic Communication & Managing Up

Adapt communication to your audience: Analytical (data, logic, evidence), Driver (results, bottom line, timeline), Expressive (vision, possibilities, stories), Amiable (impact on people, collaboration). Managing up means taking ownership of the relationship with your boss: understand their pressures and priorities, provide no surprises (bad news doesn't improve with age), speak their language, bring solutions not just problems, and make them look good—their success is your success.

> The leader who waits to be asked isn't leading—they're following. Manage up proactively.

**Framework: Audience Adaptation Grid** — Analytical (lead with data), Driver (lead with results), Expressive (lead with vision), Amiable (lead with impact on people).

**Framework: Managing Up Principles** — Understand their world, No surprises, Speak their language, Bring solutions, Make them look good.
