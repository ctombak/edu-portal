## Why Emotional Intelligence Matters

- IQ gets you hired; EI gets you promoted -- and keeps you effective
- At senior levels, technical competence is assumed; emotional intelligence differentiates
- Leaders with high EI create teams with higher engagement, lower turnover, and better results
- EI is not "being nice" -- it's having the awareness and skill to navigate human dynamics effectively

> Emotional intelligence isn't about suppressing emotions -- it's about using them intelligently.

## Goleman's Four EI Domains

- **Self-Awareness:** Knowing your emotions, triggers, strengths, and limitations
  - Can you name what you're feeling in the moment?
  - Do you know what triggers your worst reactions?
- **Self-Regulation:** Managing emotional responses rather than being controlled by them
  - Can you pause before reacting?
  - Can you choose your response rather than defaulting to habit?
- **Social Awareness:** Reading others and the emotional temperature of a room
  - Can you sense what others are feeling without them telling you?
  - Do you notice the unspoken dynamics in a meeting?
- **Relationship Management:** Using awareness to guide productive interactions
  - Can you influence without authority?
  - Can you navigate conflict and build trust across differences?

<!-- notes: Have participants rate themselves 1-10 on each domain. Most leaders are stronger on some domains than others. The development path: self-awareness first, then self-regulation, then social awareness, then relationship management. You can't manage others' emotions until you can manage your own. -->

## Trigger Mapping & The 6-Second Pause

- **Map your triggers:**
  - What situations consistently provoke a strong emotional reaction?
  - What types of people push your buttons?
  - What behaviors make you lose composure?
- **The 6-Second Pause:** Between trigger and response, there's a gap -- emotional intelligence is what you do in that gap
  1. **Name the emotion** -- "I'm feeling frustrated" (naming reduces intensity by 50%)
  2. **Pause** -- 6 seconds is enough for the amygdala to release its grip
  3. **Choose your response** -- respond from intention, not reaction

> Between stimulus and response there is a space. In that space is our freedom and our power to choose our response. -- Viktor Frankl

## Reading the Room

- Before any important meeting, ask yourself:
  - What's the energy? (High, low, tense, relaxed?)
  - Who's engaged? Who's checked out?
  - What's not being said? (The elephant in the room)
  - What emotion am I bringing into this space?
- **Signals to watch:**
  - Body language shifts when certain topics arise
  - Who speaks and who stays silent
  - Changes in tone, pace, or volume
  - Side conversations and eye contact patterns
- Observing what's below the surface separates good leaders from great ones

## Empathy as Strategic Leadership

- Empathy isn't about agreeing -- it's about understanding
- **Three types of empathy:**
  - **Cognitive empathy:** Understanding someone's perspective intellectually
  - **Emotional empathy:** Feeling what someone else feels
  - **Empathic concern:** Understanding and being moved to help
- Strategic applications:
  - Negotiation: understanding the other side's interests
  - Change management: anticipating resistance by understanding fears
  - Talent retention: seeing what your best people need before they leave
  - Conflict resolution: de-escalating by validating before problem-solving

> Empathy is not a soft skill. It's the hardest skill -- and the most strategically valuable.
