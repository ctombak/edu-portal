## The Leadership Pipeline Imperative

- The ultimate test of your leadership: what happens when you leave?
- If your team can't function without you, you haven't led -- you've created dependency
- Pipeline building is not an HR responsibility -- it's a core leadership discipline
- The organizations that win long-term are those that develop leaders faster than the competition

> A leader's legacy isn't what they built -- it's who they developed.

<!-- notes: Open with the "bus test": if you were hit by a bus tomorrow, how long would it take your team to recover? Most leaders are uncomfortable with the answer. This discomfort is the motivation for pipeline building. -->

## The 9-Box Grid

- Map team members on two dimensions: **Performance** (current results) and **Potential** (future capacity)
- **Key quadrants and actions:**
  - **Stars** (high performance, high potential) -- Invest heavily, give stretch assignments, retain at all costs
  - **High performers** (high performance, moderate potential) -- Retain, recognize, keep engaged and growing
  - **High potentials** (moderate performance, high potential) -- Develop, coach, remove barriers
  - **Solid contributors** (moderate performance, moderate potential) -- Value, develop incrementally, don't overlook
  - **Underperformers** (low performance) -- Address directly, coach or transition
- Common mistakes: confusing "quiet" with "low potential," letting bias drive assessments, ignoring solid contributors

<!-- notes: Have participants mentally place their direct reports on the grid. Then challenge them: "What evidence do you have for each placement? Would your team members agree?" The 9-box is a tool for thinking, not a label -- and it must be regularly updated. -->

## The 70-20-10 Development Model

- **70% from on-the-job experience:**
  - Stretch assignments beyond current capability
  - Leading cross-functional projects
  - Acting roles during leadership absences
  - Exposure to senior leadership meetings
- **20% from learning from others:**
  - Mentoring relationships (formal and informal)
  - Peer learning circles
  - Shadowing leaders in different functions
  - Reverse mentoring (junior teaches senior)
- **10% from formal learning:**
  - Training programs and workshops
  - Courses and certifications
  - Conferences and external exposure

<!-- notes: The key insight: most organizations over-invest in the 10% (formal training) and under-invest in the 70% (experience). Ask: "What stretch assignment could you give someone this month that would develop them more than any training course?" -->

## Development Actions You Can Take This Week

- **Delegate a leadership moment:** Let someone run the next team meeting
- **Create an "acting" opportunity:** When you're away, designate someone to lead
- **Pair junior with senior:** On a specific project, not generically
- **Debrief together after key moments:** "What did you notice? What would you do differently?"
- **Share your thinking:** Explain WHY you made a decision, not just what you decided
- **Give visibility:** Bring a high-potential into a senior meeting and let them present

> Development isn't something you add to the work -- it's how you design the work.
