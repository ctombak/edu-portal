## Burnout Prevention & Sustainable Pace

## Engineering-Specific Burnout Risks

- Engineering teams face unique pressures:
  - **On-call rotations:** Sleep disruption, constant alertness, incident fatigue
  - **Incident response:** High-stakes, high-adrenaline events that accumulate stress
  - **"Always shipping" culture:** Continuous deployment means continuous pressure
  - **Context switching:** Meetings, code reviews, incidents, features -- fragmented attention
  - **AI reshaping the profession:** Existential anxiety about career relevance
- Extending Module 1's energy management: burnout in engineering is often normalized as "that's just how it is"
- It's not. It's a leadership failure.

> Burnout isn't a badge of honor. It's a sign that the system is broken -- and fixing the system is your job.

## Monitoring Team Health

- **Leading indicators of burnout (catch it early):**
  - Decline in code review quality or engagement
  - Increasing cynicism in retros and meetings
  - Reduced participation in voluntary activities (hack days, tech talks)
  - More frequent sick days or late starts
  - Withdrawal from social interactions
  - Declining PR quality or increasing shortcuts
- **Structured check-ins:**
  - Weekly 1:1s with a genuine "How are you doing?" (not just status updates)
  - Monthly team health surveys (anonymous, 3-5 questions)
  - Quarterly retrospectives focused on sustainability, not just delivery
- The signal is in the trend, not the snapshot -- track over time

## Creating Sustainable Pace

- **Practical tools for engineering managers:**
  - **On-call reform:** Adequate rotation, follow-the-sun where possible, compensatory time off after incidents
  - **Meeting-free blocks:** Protect at least 4 hours of uninterrupted deep work daily
  - **Sprint capacity:** Never plan at 100% -- build in buffer for incidents, support, and learning
  - **Celebration of completion:** Acknowledge shipped work before immediately pivoting to the next sprint
  - **Post-incident care:** After major incidents, allow recovery time -- don't just jump back to feature work
- **The sustainable pace test:** Can your team maintain this intensity for the next 6 months? If not, something needs to change now.

## Navigating AI Anxiety

- AI is reshaping the engineering profession in real time
- Team fears are real and legitimate:
  - "Will AI replace my job?"
  - "Am I learning skills that will be obsolete?"
  - "I'm not keeping up with AI tools and I feel behind"
- **The manager's role:**
  - Acknowledge the anxiety -- don't dismiss it
  - Reframe AI as an amplifier of engineering judgment, not a replacement
  - Create psychological safety around AI adoption and experimentation
  - Allocate learning time specifically for AI tools
  - Share your own uncertainty -- "I don't have all the answers either, but here's how I'm thinking about it"

> AI changes the tools, not the fundamentals. Problem decomposition, system thinking, and judgment under uncertainty become MORE valuable, not less.
