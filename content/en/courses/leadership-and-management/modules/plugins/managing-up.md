# Managing Up & Across
## Building Strategic Relationships with Bosses & Peers

## Boss Mapping Exercise

- Map your boss's world to manage them effectively:
  - **Top pressures:** What keeps them up at night?
  - **Information preferences:** Detail or summary? Written or verbal? Scheduled or ad hoc?
  - **Stress triggers:** What causes them to micromanage or withdraw?
  - **What they value in reports:** Initiative? Thoroughness? Speed? Loyalty?
  - **Where they feel exposed:** What are they worried about looking bad on?
- The principle: manage your boss the way THEY want to be managed, not the way YOU would want to be managed

> Understanding your boss's pressures isn't flattery -- it's strategic empathy.

## The COIN Framework for Upward Feedback

- Giving feedback to your boss requires courage and skill:
  - **Context:** Set the stage -- "I want to share something because I think it will help the team"
  - **Observation:** What you noticed, without judgment -- "In the last two meetings, I noticed..."
  - **Impact:** The effect you observed -- "The result was that the team..."
  - **Next:** A suggestion or question -- "Would it help if we..." or "What do you think about..."
- Frame upward feedback as helping the team, not criticizing the boss
- Choose your moment -- not when they're stressed or in public
- One topic at a time -- don't stack feedback

<!-- notes: Role-play exercise: have participants practice giving upward feedback using COIN. The key coaching point: tone matters more than words. Curious and helpful beats critical and righteous every time. -->

## Peer Network Audit

- Rate your relationships with 5-7 key peers on a 1-5 scale:
  - 1 = Adversarial or non-existent
  - 3 = Functional but transactional
  - 5 = Strategic partnership with mutual trust
- **Strategies for building peer alliances:**
  - Find shared interests and common goals
  - Offer help first -- build credit before you need to withdraw
  - Create informal touchpoints (coffee, lunch, quick check-ins)
  - Share information generously -- hoarding information erodes trust
  - Give credit publicly and take blame privately

> Your peer network is your early warning system, your support structure, and your coalition-building foundation.

<!-- notes: Have participants complete the audit and identify one peer relationship to invest in this month. The insight: most leaders invest heavily in managing up and down but neglect lateral relationships -- which often determine whether their initiatives succeed or fail. -->

## When Styles Clash

- What to do when your boss's leadership style conflicts with yours:
  - **Recognize the pattern** -- is this a style difference or a values conflict?
  - **Adapt first** -- flex your style before asking them to change
  - **Find the value** -- what does their style offer that yours doesn't?
  - **Set boundaries** -- adaptation has limits; protect your integrity
  - **Seek to understand** -- their style was shaped by experiences you may not know about
- The goal isn't to become someone else -- it's to build enough range to work effectively with different styles

<!-- notes: Close with this reframe: the boss who frustrates you most may be the one who stretches your leadership range the most. Adaptability isn't weakness -- it's advanced leadership. -->
