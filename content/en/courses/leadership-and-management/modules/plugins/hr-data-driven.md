# Data-Driven HR
## People Analytics for Strategic Influence

<!-- notes: Builds capability to use people data as forward-looking business storytelling rather than backward-looking reporting. Data is HR's most underused source of strategic influence. -->

## From Reporting to Storytelling

- The shift most HR teams need to make:
  - **Old:** "Here are the turnover numbers" (backward-looking, descriptive)
  - **New:** "Here's what turnover patterns tell us about our ability to deliver on next year's strategy" (forward-looking, prescriptive)
- Data without narrative is noise; narrative without data is opinion
- The goal: use people data to predict, not just to react
- **The storytelling structure:**
  - What's happening? (The data)
  - So what? (The business implication)
  - Now what? (The recommended action)

> Numbers don't influence decisions -- stories built on numbers do.

## People Analytics for Influence

- Connecting to Module 2's influence and stakeholder mastery frameworks:
  - **Know your audience:** CFO wants cost impact; CEO wants strategic risk; COO wants operational efficiency
  - **Lead with business questions, not HR metrics:** "Can we deliver on our growth targets with current attrition rates?" not "Turnover is at 18%"
  - **Benchmark externally:** Internal data alone lacks urgency; external comparisons create it
  - **Predict, don't just report:** Use trend data to forecast -- "If this pattern continues, by Q3 we'll face..."
- The influence advantage: when HR brings data that other functions don't have, they become indispensable

## The Metrics That Matter

- Not all people data is equally valuable -- focus on metrics that connect to strategic priorities
- **The essential people metrics:**
  - **Quality of hire:** Are new hires performing and staying? (Connects to business capability)
  - **Regrettable turnover:** Are the RIGHT people leaving? (Connects to competitive risk)
  - **Manager effectiveness:** How do teams perform by manager? (Connects to organizational health)
  - **Internal mobility:** Are people growing or stagnating? (Connects to pipeline strength)
  - **Time to productivity:** How quickly do new hires contribute? (Connects to operational efficiency)
- The discipline: identify the 3-5 metrics that matter most for YOUR organization's strategy and ignore the rest

> The most dangerous people analytics habit is measuring everything and acting on nothing.

## Building Your Analytics Capability

- You don't need a data science team to start:
  - **Level 1 - Descriptive:** What happened? (Basic dashboards, trend reports)
  - **Level 2 - Diagnostic:** Why did it happen? (Correlation analysis, exit interview themes)
  - **Level 3 - Predictive:** What will happen? (Turnover risk models, workforce planning)
  - **Level 4 - Prescriptive:** What should we do? (Scenario modeling, recommendation engines)
- Start where you are and build incrementally
- The biggest barrier isn't technology -- it's the habit of presenting data without insight

> You don't need perfect data to tell a compelling story. You need the right data, presented with clarity and business context.
