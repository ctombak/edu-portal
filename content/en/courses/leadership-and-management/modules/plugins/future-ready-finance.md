# Building a Future-Ready Finance Team
## Talent, Skills & AI Fluency for Finance

<!-- notes: Helps finance leaders build teams that can thrive in 2026 -- navigating shrinking talent pipelines and expanding skill requirements. -->

---

## The Finance Talent Squeeze

- The pipeline challenge: fewer graduates entering traditional finance while required skills are expanding
- **Skills that were sufficient:** Accounting accuracy, compliance knowledge, spreadsheet proficiency
- **Skills now essential:** Data analytics, AI fluency, business partnering, strategic communication, change leadership
- The competition for hybrid talent (finance + technology + business acumen) is fierce
- Retention is as critical as recruitment -- developing existing talent is faster and cheaper than hiring

> The finance team of 2030 will look nothing like the finance team of 2020. The leaders who start building now will have an insurmountable advantage.

<!-- notes: Ask: "What skills does your team need in 3 years that it doesn't have today?" Most finance leaders can list 5-10 gaps. The question becomes: are you building those capabilities now, or hoping they'll appear when needed? -->

## Future-Ready Development

- Connecting to Plugin D (Leadership Pipeline) and Module 5 (AI Fluency):
  - **70% on-the-job:** Rotate team members through business partnering roles, stretch assignments in analytics, cross-functional projects
  - **20% from others:** Pair finance team members with technology, strategy, or business unit colleagues for mutual learning
  - **10% formal:** Targeted training in data visualization, AI tools, and strategic communication
- **The development priorities for finance teams:**
  - Data storytelling and visualization
  - Business partnering and advisory skills
  - AI tool proficiency for finance applications
  - Strategic thinking beyond number-crunching
  - Change leadership and influence skills

<!-- notes: Have participants identify the top 3 development priorities for their team and one concrete action for each. The 70-20-10 model is especially powerful in finance because the best learning happens when finance professionals work alongside the business, not in isolation. -->

## AI Fluency in Finance

- Building practical AI literacy across finance teams:
  - **Automated reconciliation:** Reduce manual matching and exception handling
  - **Predictive analytics:** Move from descriptive ("what happened") to predictive ("what will happen")
  - **Natural language reporting:** Generate narrative commentary on financial results
  - **Anomaly detection:** Identify unusual patterns that warrant investigation
  - **Scenario modeling:** Run multiple financial scenarios in minutes instead of days
- The human judgment that AI cannot replace: ethical reasoning, relationship context, strategic trade-offs, stakeholder navigation

<!-- notes: The key message: AI in finance isn't about replacing people -- it's about freeing them from routine work to focus on judgment, relationships, and strategy. Show a concrete example: what used to take 3 days of manual analysis can now be done in 30 minutes, freeing the team for advisory work. -->

## Building a Learning Culture in Finance

- Finance teams often resist change due to high stakes of accuracy and compliance
- **Creating safety to learn:**
  - Allocate "experimentation time" -- even 2 hours per week for new tools and approaches
  - Celebrate learning, not just accuracy -- reward team members who try new approaches
  - Create internal "champions" who adopt new tools first and mentor others
  - Share failures openly -- "I tried X and it didn't work because..." normalizes learning
- **The retention equation:** Top finance talent stays where they're growing; they leave where they're stagnating

> The best finance teams aren't the ones with the most tools -- they're the ones with the most curiosity.
