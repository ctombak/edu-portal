## The 2026 Leadership Landscape

- **71%** of leaders report increased stress compared to three years ago (DDI Global Leadership Forecast)
- **40%** have considered stepping back from leadership entirely
- **75%** report burnout symptoms at least monthly
- Organizations are flattening -- fewer layers, wider spans of control
- Leaders' influence extends **3x** further than a decade ago
- Positional authority matters less; **relational capital** matters more
- AI is reshaping decision-making while human connection becomes more critical

> In our culture, the leader is often seen as the "parent" who has all the answers. Today, we look at why the best global leaders don't have all the answers -- they have the best questions.

## The Shifting Leadership Contract

- **Old contract:** Leader as expert, commander, problem-solver
- **New contract:** Leader as coach, connector, sense-maker
- What hasn't changed: people still need **direction**, **support**, and **meaning**
- What has changed: how those needs are met

| Old Paradigm | New Paradigm |
|---|---|
| Command and control | Coach and enable |
| Information hoarding | Radical transparency |
| Individual heroics | Collective intelligence |
| Avoiding failure | Learning from failure |
| Annual reviews | Continuous feedback |

<!-- notes: This table is powerful because most participants operate somewhere in between. Ask: "Where does your organization sit on this spectrum? Where do YOU sit?" Acknowledge that the shift is real but uneven -- most of us toggle between old and new daily. -->

## Leadership Crucibles: The Moments That Forge Leaders

- Warren Bennis and Robert Thomas coined the term **"leadership crucibles"**
- A crucible is a transformative experience through which a person comes to a new or altered sense of identity
- These are the moments -- failures, crises, conflicts, ethical dilemmas -- that forge who you are as a leader
- Crucibles are not comfortable, but they are where the deepest learning happens
- Research shows that how leaders **make meaning** from crucible experiences separates extraordinary leaders from average ones

> One of the most reliable indicators of true leadership is an individual's ability to find meaning in negative events and to learn from even the most trying circumstances. -- Warren Bennis

<!-- notes: Set this up carefully. You're about to ask people to be vulnerable. Normalize that crucibles are often painful -- failures, losses, conflicts. Share your own crucible briefly (1 minute) to model the depth you're asking for. -->

## Exercise: Sharing Your Crucible Story

- **Step 1 -- Individual reflection (3 minutes):**
  - Think of a formative leadership moment -- a failure, conflict, crisis, or unexpected challenge that changed how you lead
  - What happened? What did you learn? How did it shape you?
- **Step 2 -- Pair share (5 minutes):**
  - Turn to the person next to you
  - Each person shares their crucible story (2.5 minutes each)
  - Listener: just listen. No advice, no fixing -- just witness
- **Step 3 -- Group debrief (5 minutes):**
  - What themes emerged across your stories?

## Crucible Themes: What We Heard

- Common themes from leadership crucibles research and practice:
  - **Resilience** is built, not born -- it comes from surviving difficulty
  - **Relationships** are the foundation -- every crucible involves other people
  - **Self-awareness** deepens through struggle, not success
  - **Values** are clarified when they are tested
  - **Humility** opens the door to growth
- Your crucible stories are not just your past -- they are the foundation of your leadership identity

## The Day's Framework: Three Leadership Capacities

- Today we build three interconnected capacities:
  1. **The Adaptive Leader** -- reading context, managing yourself, diagnosing problems accurately
  2. **The Relational Leader** -- building influence, navigating stakeholders, managing up
  3. **The Growth Leader** -- coaching others, creating safety, delivering feedback
- These are not separate skills -- they are an integrated system
- The best leaders flex across all three depending on what the moment demands
