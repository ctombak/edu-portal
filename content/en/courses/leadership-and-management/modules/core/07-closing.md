## The Day in Review: Three Leadership Capacities

- We have built three interconnected capacities today:
  1. **The Adaptive Leader** -- diagnosing context, managing energy, leading yourself first
  2. **The Relational Leader** -- building influence, navigating stakeholders, managing up
  3. **The Growth Leader** -- creating safety, coaching with questions, giving feedback that works
- These are not separate modules -- they are an **integrated system**
- The strongest leaders flex across all three, adapting to what the moment demands

> Leadership is not a position. It is the daily practice of these three capacities.

## The Transfer Problem: Why Training Doesn't Stick

- Research from Brinkerhoff: only **15%** of training transfers to on-the-job behavior without follow-up support
- The three killers of training transfer:
  1. **No specific plan** -- "I'll be a better leader" is not a plan
  2. **No accountability** -- without someone checking in, old habits win
  3. **No early wins** -- if people don't see results in the first two weeks, they abandon the new behavior
- The next 30 minutes are designed to beat these odds
- You will leave with: a **written plan**, an **accountability partner**, and a **48-hour first action**

<!-- notes: This slide creates urgency for the action planning. The Brinkerhoff statistic (15% transfer rate) is sobering but motivating -- it means the action plan is not optional, it's essential. Frame it positively: "You've invested a full day in this. The action plan is what turns that investment into results." -->

## The 100-Day Action Plan

- Research shows that **100 days** is the optimal horizon for leadership behavior change -- long enough for new habits to form, short enough to maintain urgency
- Your plan covers three horizons:
  - **48 Hours:** One immediate action (the hardest step is the first)
  - **30 Days:** Two to three sustained practices
  - **100 Days:** One measurable leadership outcome
- The plan must be:
  - **Specific** -- "Have a GROW coaching conversation with Alex by Friday" not "coach more"
  - **Observable** -- someone else could verify you did it
  - **Meaningful** -- connected to your real challenges, not hypothetical ones
  - **Accountable** -- someone will check on you

<!-- notes: Emphasize specificity ruthlessly. "Be a better leader" is not a plan. "Have a coaching conversation with Sarah using GROW by Friday and ask my team for one piece of feedback by next Wednesday" is a plan. The 100-day horizon is based on research on habit formation and organizational change cycles. -->

## Writing Your Action Plan -- Part 1: Start, Stop, Continue

- In your workbook, complete each section:
- **I WILL START (new practices):**
  - Practice 1: ___
  - Practice 2: ___
  - When I will start: ___
- **I WILL STOP (habits that aren't serving me):**
  - Habit 1: ___
  - What I will do instead: ___
- **I WILL CONTINUE (strengths to leverage more intentionally):**
  - Strength: ___
  - How I will leverage it: ___
- **I WILL DELEGATE (to develop others):**
  - Task/responsibility: ___
  - To whom: ___
  - By when: ___

<!-- notes: Give 5 minutes for this section. Walk the room and coach on specificity. Common "starts": coaching conversations, asking for feedback, stakeholder mapping. Common "stops": solving problems for others, avoiding difficult conversations, micromanaging. Common "continues": building relationships, strategic thinking. The "delegate" section connects to Module 1's "give the work back" practice. -->

## Writing Your Action Plan -- Part 2: The 48-Hour Commitment

- The most critical part of your plan: **What will you do in the next 48 hours?**
- Your 48-hour action must be:
  - Completable within two business days
  - Specific enough that you know exactly when it's done
  - Connected to today's learning
  - Slightly uncomfortable (if it's easy, it's not growth)
- **Examples of strong 48-hour actions:**
  - "Ask my team in Monday's meeting: 'What is one thing I could do differently to make you more effective?'"
  - "Have a 15-minute GROW coaching conversation with Priya about her project challenge"
  - "Give SBI reinforcing feedback to two team members who went above and beyond last week"
  - "Map stakeholders for the Q3 initiative using the Power-Interest Grid"
  - "Schedule a managing-up conversation with my boss to understand their top 3 priorities"

<!-- notes: The 48-hour window is deliberate. Research on implementation intentions (Peter Gollwitzer) shows that specific time-bound commitments dramatically increase follow-through. Ask each participant to write their 48-hour action and circle it. This is the single most important output of the day. -->

## Writing Your Action Plan -- Part 3: The 30-Day and 100-Day View

- **My 30-Day Practices (what I will do consistently for the next month):**
  1. ___
  2. ___
  3. ___
- **My 30-Day Check-In:** Date: ___ Method: ___ (meeting, call, email with accountability partner)
- **My 100-Day Leadership Outcome (what will be different because of these practices):**
  - Outcome: ___
  - How I will measure it: ___
  - How I will know I have succeeded: ___
- **Time: 5 minutes to complete**

<!-- notes: The 100-day outcome should be observable and meaningful. Not "I will be a better coach" but "My team will be making more decisions independently, and I will be spending 20% less time solving problems for them." Help participants think in terms of outcomes, not just activities. The 30-day check-in date must be specific: "March 15, 10am, coffee with Maria" not "sometime next month." -->

## Accountability Partnerships

- Find your **accountability partner** -- someone from today's training you trust and respect
- **Exchange:**
  - Your 48-hour action
  - Your 30-day practices
  - Your preferred method of check-in (text, email, call, coffee)
- **Agree on:**
  - A **30-day check-in** date and time (schedule it now, in both calendars)
  - A **100-day check-in** date and time
  - Permission to be **honest** -- your partner's job is to hold you accountable, not to let you off the hook
- **The data:** Research from the American Society of Training & Development found that having an accountability partner increases the probability of completing a goal from **65% to 95%**

<!-- notes: Give 5 minutes for this. Participants should pair up, exchange plans, and literally put calendar invites in their phones. The 65% to 95% statistic is powerful motivation. Walk the room and ensure everyone has a partner. If there's an odd number, create one group of three. The key: the check-in must be scheduled NOW, not "we'll figure it out later." -->

## Key Takeaway Sharing

- We go around the room. Each person shares:
  1. Their **single biggest insight** from today (one sentence)
  2. Their **48-hour action** (one sentence)
- **Rules:** One sentence each, no preambles. Keep it tight.
- This creates **public commitment** -- research by Robert Cialdini shows that public commitments are significantly more likely to be followed through than private ones

<!-- notes: This is a powerful closing ritual. Go around the room systematically -- no one opts out. The one-sentence constraint forces clarity.
