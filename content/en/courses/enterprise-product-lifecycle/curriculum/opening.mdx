Establish connection between participants, set the frame for the day, and surface collective wisdom through shared product delivery experiences—both successes and spectacular failures.

## The Enterprise Product Reality in 2026

70% of enterprise IT projects experience significant scope creep. Only 29% are completed on time, on budget, and with required features. The average enterprise manages 300+ applications, with integration complexity growing exponentially. Products increasingly span hardware and software boundaries—IoT, edge computing, and embedded systems are becoming mainstream enterprise concerns. Yet most organizations still treat product delivery as a linear, purely-software exercise.

> The biggest risk in enterprise product delivery isn't technical failure—it's building the wrong thing well.

## Product Delivery War Stories

Participants share formative product delivery experiences—the projects that went sideways, the vendors that under-delivered, the architectures that couldn't scale, and the launches that succeeded against the odds. These stories reveal patterns: the same failure modes repeat across industries, technologies, and decades. The session surfaces collective themes about what separates successful enterprise products from expensive failures.
