## The Enterprise Product Reality in 2026

- 70% of enterprise IT projects experience significant scope creep
- Only 29% are completed on time, on budget, and with required features
- The average enterprise manages 300+ applications, with integration complexity growing exponentially
- Products increasingly span hardware and software boundaries -- IoT, edge computing, and embedded systems are becoming mainstream enterprise concerns
- Most organizations still treat product delivery as a linear, purely-software exercise

> The biggest risk in enterprise product delivery isn't technical failure -- it's building the wrong thing well.

<!-- notes: These statistics set the stage for why structured lifecycle management matters. Let the numbers sink in before moving on. -->

---

## Product Delivery War Stories

- Participants share formative product delivery experiences
- The projects that went sideways, the vendors that under-delivered, the architectures that couldn't scale, and the launches that succeeded against the odds
- These stories reveal patterns: the same failure modes repeat across industries, technologies, and decades
- The session surfaces collective themes about what separates successful enterprise products from expensive failures

> Use this exercise to build trust in the room. Vulnerability about past failures creates the psychological safety needed for honest learning throughout the day.
